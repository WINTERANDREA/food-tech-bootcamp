### Q2. What SAO do you want to build with us? What tools would the agent need? Why does this need to be a SAO and not simulation?

I want to build the **first AI-operated artisanal DOP food producer**.

Market was vending. Retail was Luna. The next frontier is production — a regulated denomination of origin workshop at Tosi-level scale (~3 tons/day). The agent carries the 20-founder load: procurement, compliance, pricing, finance, sales, brand, phone voice. Humans keep the hands — curd, salting, aging caves. AI orchestrates, humans deliver.

**Why it has to be real and not simulated:**

- **Biological time can't be faked.** A wheel of Gorgonzola ages 80–120 days. The agent's decisions on milk, salting and cave rotation only show up as finished product months later.
- **DOP oversight is human and relationship-based.** Consortium inspectors arrive unannounced. The relationships between producers, inspectors and boards go back generations. A simulator can't reproduce this.
- **Italian B2B runs on voice.** Small restaurants phone the caseificio; they don't email. The agent has to pick up in Italian, handle dialect, and survive the small talk before the order lands.
- **Food-safety liability is real.** Every kilo shipped carries consumer-health exposure. HACCP, pathogen panels, per-batch traceability fail in ways you only see once real product reaches real customers.

**Tools the agent needs:**
Italian phone/WhatsApp/email, Italian bureaucracy (SDI, ASL, DOP consortium), production telemetry, HACCP + pathogen panels + per-batch traceability, seasonal cashflow, procurement, e-commerce + chef relationships.

**What I bring:**
A live commercial layer proof shipping out of Tosi, the rare gastronome who codes and knows food safety, with a network that opens doors on the excellence that don't surface on Google.
