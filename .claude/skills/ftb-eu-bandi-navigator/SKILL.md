# EU Bandi Navigator — Funding Skill

## Purpose

Identify, evaluate, and prepare applications for EU, Italian national, and regional funding opportunities relevant to FTB's mission. Turn "there are bandi available" into "here's exactly which one to apply for, by when, with what consortium, and with what narrative."

## Evaluation Framework — Should We Apply?

Before spending time on any bando, score it:

```
BANDO: [Name]
PROGRAM: [EIT Food / Horizon / Regional / etc.]
DEADLINE: [Date]
BUDGET AVAILABLE: [Total call budget]
MAX GRANT PER PROJECT: [Amount]
CO-FUNDING REQUIRED: [% or amount]

SCORING:
Mission alignment:     [1-5] Does it fund what FTB actually does?
Effort vs reward:      [1-5] Grant size vs application complexity?
Win probability:       [1-5] How competitive? Do we meet all criteria?
Timeline fit:          [1-5] Does the deadline work with our capacity?
Partnership ready:     [1-5] Do we have the required consortium?
Multi-output:          [1-5] Does applying produce other value (even if we lose)?

TOTAL: [/30]

DECISION:
> 24: Apply. Priority.
20-24: Apply if time allows.
15-19: Consider. Prepare but only submit if quality is high.
< 15: Skip. Don't waste the time.
```

### The Multi-Output Rule

Even a losing application has value IF it produces:

- A polished project description (reusable for other calls)
- A consortium relationship (UNISG, other partners)
- Market research and data compilation
- A narrative document usable for Anthropic, investors, or clients

If applying produces zero secondary value, the threshold for "apply" should be higher.

## Application Writing — The FTB Narrative Framework

### The Core Story (Adapt Per Program)

Every application tells the same story in different languages:

**Paragraph 1 — The Crisis:**
Italy has 300,000 artisanal food producers — casari, panificatori, oleari, vignaioli, norcini, pastai. They produce the most respected food in the world under protected designations (DOP, IGP, DOC). 98% are SMEs with fewer than 10 employees. They compete against industrial producers with digital infrastructure, automated commercial processes, and marketing budgets that artisanal producers cannot match. The result: declining competitiveness, closures, and irreversible loss of cultural and gastronomic heritage.

**Paragraph 2 — The Gap:**
Existing digital transformation programs target generic SME needs (websites, social media, basic e-commerce). Artisanal food producers require domain-specific tools that understand their production processes, regulatory frameworks (HACCP, DOP/IGP certification), supply chain dynamics, and commercial contexts. Generic CRM, generic AI chatbots, and generic marketing tools fail because they don't speak the language of the producer.

**Paragraph 3 — The Solution:**
Food Tech Bootcamp develops AI-native tools specifically designed for the artisanal food sector. Built on Claude (Anthropic), our solutions are designed by a team that combines gastronomic sciences education (UNISG Pollenzo) with software development expertise. Each tool is built FROM the production floor — in collaboration with real producers — not imposed from outside. Current applications: AI-powered commercial intelligence for a DOP caseificio (prospect identification, script generation, follow-up coordination), food safety compliance automation (HACCP), product development acceleration for food entrepreneurs.

**Paragraph 4 — The Evidence:**
[Insert Tosi experiment results + any other data. Quantify everything.]

**Paragraph 5 — The Team & Consortium:**
[FTB founder credentials + UNISG partnership + Anthropic technology + producer partners]

**Paragraph 6 — The Impact:**
[Projected impact at scale. How many producers could benefit? What's the economic multiplier? What heritage is preserved?]

### Adapt the Language Per Evaluator

| Evaluator            | Emphasize                                                                        | Avoid                                               |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **EIT Food**         | Food system transformation, startup methodology, scalability, market readiness   | Academic jargon, pure research language             |
| **Horizon Europe**   | Research novelty, consortium quality, EU-wide impact, interdisciplinary approach | Commercial language, product pitches                |
| **EIC Accelerator**  | Market potential, team capability, deep-tech innovation, business model          | Academic framing, incremental innovation            |
| **Italian national** | Made in Italy competitiveness, territorial development, employment, SME support  | English buzzwords, disconnection from local economy |
| **Regional**         | Local economic impact, specific territory development, job creation              | Abstract innovation language, global scale claims   |

## EU Evaluation Criteria — What Scores Well

### Horizon Europe / EIT Food

**Excellence (Score weight: ~33%)**

- Novelty of approach (AI specifically designed for artisanal food = novel)
- Quality of research methodology
- Ambition and feasibility balance

**Impact (Score weight: ~33%)**

- Quantified expected outcomes (number of producers served, revenue increase, time saved)
- Contribution to EU policy objectives (Farm to Fork, Digital Europe, AI Act compliance)
- Dissemination and exploitation plan

**Implementation (Score weight: ~33%)**

- Quality of consortium (university + industry + SME = ideal triangle)
- Work plan clarity and deliverables
- Budget justification and resource allocation
- Risk management

### What ALWAYS Scores Well in Food/Agri Calls

- Human-centric AI (aligns with EU AI Act philosophy)
- University-industry collaboration (especially UNISG = unique asset)
- Real SME involvement (actual producers as project partners, not just "beneficiaries")
- Sustainability metrics (waste reduction, resource efficiency, carbon footprint)
- Gender dimension (if applicable — and often is in artisanal food)
- Multi-country potential (Italian model exportable to other EU artisanal food regions)
- Cultural heritage preservation (powerful in European context)
- Open science / open data commitments

### What ALWAYS Scores Poorly

- Generic digital transformation without domain specificity
- Technology-first proposals that don't demonstrate user understanding
- Solo applicants without partnerships (especially without academic partner)
- Proposals without concrete evidence or pilot data
- Overpromising on impact without evidence base
- Ignoring ethical, gender, or environmental dimensions

## Consortium Building

### The Ideal FTB Consortium

```
FTB (Technology partner / SME)
    → AI development, product design, artisan coordination

UNISG Pollenzo (Academic partner)
    → Research methodology, food science validation, training design

Artisanal Producer(s) (End-user partner)
    → Real-world testing environment, domain expertise, market validation

[Optional] Second university or research center
    → If multi-country required (e.g., Prof. Banglar in India, or a French/Spanish food university)

[Optional] Industry association
    → Consorzio DOP, Slow Food, regional food association → for dissemination and scale
```

### How to Approach UNISG for Consortium

1. Start with personal alumni connection
2. Identify specific UNISG researchers whose work aligns (Pollenzo Food Lab, Circular Economy for Food)
3. Propose a specific call and a specific role for UNISG
4. Offer to write their section of the proposal (reduce their effort)
5. Frame it as mutual benefit: "Your research + our technology + real producers = fundable project"

### How to Approach Producers for Consortium

1. They don't need to DO research — they provide the testing environment
2. Frame their participation as: "You get [specific tool/outcome] built for you, funded by EU, at no cost"
3. Their commitment: time for interviews, access to operations, feedback on tools, letter of support
4. Keep their administrative burden near zero

## Application Timeline Management

### Standard EU Call Timeline

```
Call opens:          Week 0
Consortium forming:  Week 0-4 (start BEFORE call opens if possible)
Concept development: Week 4-8
Writing:             Week 8-14
Internal review:     Week 14-16
Submission:          Week 16-18
Evaluation:          Week 18-34 (4-6 months)
Results:             Week 34+
Project start:       ~6 months after results
```

### Minimum Time Needed Per Application Type

| Type                             | Time investment | Can do alongside Cortilia? |
| -------------------------------- | --------------- | -------------------------- |
| Camera di Commercio voucher      | 8-15 hours      | Yes                        |
| EIT Food Innovation Prize        | 10-20 hours     | Yes                        |
| EIT Food Startup-Corporate       | 20-40 hours     | Tight but yes              |
| Regional innovation voucher      | 15-25 hours     | Yes                        |
| EIC Accelerator (short proposal) | 40-80 hours     | Need dedicated time        |
| Horizon Europe (full proposal)   | 100-200 hours   | Needs UNISG to co-write    |

### The Realistic Rule

With a full-time job at Cortilia and the Tosi project running, capacity for funding applications is ~10-15 hours/month. This means:

- Q2 2026: ONE small application (Innovation Prize or Camera di Commercio)
- Q3 2026: ONE medium application (EIT Food Startup-Corporate with Tosi)
- Q4 2026: Prepare EIC Accelerator short proposal (only if SRL by then)
- 2027: Horizon Europe only if UNISG partnership is formalized and they lead writing

## Quick Reference — Bando Language Translation

| What they say                          | What it means                                        | FTB equivalent                                                 |
| -------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Technology Readiness Level (TRL) 4-6" | Prototype to demonstrated in relevant environment    | We have working tools tested with real producers               |
| "Co-creation with end-users"           | Build WITH the users, not FOR them                   | We literally work in the caseificio                            |
| "Human-centric AI"                     | AI that augments humans, not replaces them           | Our entire philosophy — the artisan is always in the loop      |
| "Responsible innovation"               | Ethical, inclusive, sustainable                      | We preserve heritage, not disrupt it                           |
| "Cascade funding"                      | Money flows through a bigger project to smaller ones | Look for open calls FROM funded projects (UNISG's NODES, etc.) |
| "Letter of Intent (LOI)"               | A partner confirming they'll participate             | Get this from Tosi, UNISG, any producer partner                |
| "Dissemination and exploitation"       | How you'll spread results and make them sustainable  | Blog, meetups, UNISG workshops, open-source tools              |
| "Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)"    | Measurable success metrics                           | Number of producers served, revenue increase %, time saved     |
