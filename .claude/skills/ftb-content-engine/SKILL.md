# FTB Content Engine

## Content Hierarchy (Priority Order)

1. **Project pages** (website) — Show the work. Andon Labs format.
2. **Blog posts** (1-2/month) — Thought leadership + SEO
3. **LinkedIn posts** (2-3/week) — Network building + visibility
4. **Case study documents** (for partners) — Anthropic, UNISG, EU evaluators
5. **Presentation slides** (as needed) — Meetups, pitches, workshops

### The Rule

Never create content that doesn't reference a REAL project, a REAL number, or a REAL artisan. Abstract thought leadership without grounding = generic agency content = the opposite of Manrique.

## Blog Posts

### Structure

```
TITLE: Bold question or statement. Max 10 words.

OPENING: 1-2 sentences. Strongest claim or most surprising fact.
No preamble, no "In today's rapidly evolving..." — start with impact.

THE PROBLEM: 1 paragraph from the artisan's perspective.
Concrete: a specific producer, a specific challenge, specific numbers.

WHAT WE DID / DISCOVERED: 2-3 paragraphs. Technical enough to be
credible, accessible enough that a producer understands value.
At least one specific metric or outcome.

WHAT WE LEARNED: 1-2 paragraphs. Failures and surprises are more
compelling than smooth success stories. Be honest.

THE BIGGER PICTURE: 1 paragraph. Connect back to the 300,000.

CLOSE: 1-2 sentences. A statement, not a CTA. Manrique didn't say
"visit my building." He built something you couldn't NOT visit.
```

### Length

600-1200 words. Never longer. If it needs more, split into a series.

### Topic Templates

**The Experiment Report:**
"Can AI [do specific thing] for [specific type of artisan]?"
→ Setup, process, results, learnings.

**The Industry Insight:**
"Why [surprising fact about artisanal food sector]"
→ Data-driven, one core insight, connected to what FTB builds.

**The Tool Breakdown:**
"How we built [specific tool] for [specific producer]"
→ Technical but grounded. Show the code serving the craft.

**The Philosophy Piece:**
"[Manrique-style statement about food, technology, identity]"
→ Rare (1 every 2-3 months). Must be earned by real work.

### Sample Openings

❌ "In today's competitive food industry, artisanal producers face unprecedented challenges in digital transformation..."

✅ "Andrea makes the best gorgonzola in the world. His sales team is three people. His competitor's is thirty."

❌ "We recently completed an exciting project exploring the intersection of AI and traditional food production..."

✅ "We asked Claude to sell cheese. Here's what happened."

❌ "The Italian artisanal food sector represents a significant opportunity for technological innovation..."

✅ "300,000 artisanal food producers in Italy. 98% without a CRM. Every year, some close — and their knowledge disappears forever."

## LinkedIn Posts

### Format

Short. 3-8 lines visible before "see more." Hook in line 1. One idea per post. End with a statement, not a question begging for engagement.

### Templates

**The Number:**

```
[Striking statistic]
[One sentence of context]
[What FTB does about it]
```

**The Learning:**

```
[Something unexpected from a project]
[What we expected vs what happened]
[The insight — 1-2 sentences]
```

**The Artisan Story:**

```
[A producer's reality in 2-3 sentences]
[The gap between their product quality and their digital tools]
[One sentence about what we built / are building]
```

**The Manrique Quote:**

```
"[Manifesto-style statement]"

[2-3 sentences connecting it to food tech reality]
```

### Samples

```
3 people sell the best gorgonzola in the world.
Their competitor has 30 salespeople and a CRM.

We built an AI layer that turns 3 into the equivalent of 15.
Not by replacing anyone — by making every conversation smarter,
every follow-up timely, every prospect researched.

Week 3 results: [metrics when available]
```

```
I studied gastronomy at Pollenzo. Then I went to India to learn
to code. People thought I was confused.

8 years later, AI is transforming every industry — including food.
The gastronomes who can code will build the tools.
The coders who understand food will know what to build.

I'm both. That's Food Tech Bootcamp.
```

```
A cheesemaker doesn't need a website.
A baker doesn't need a social media strategy.
An olive oil producer doesn't need a rebrand.

They need to find the 47 restaurants in Milan that would love
their product — and reach them before the industrial alternative
arrives first.

That's what AI should do for food.
```

### Rules

- Never use hashtags
- Never ask engagement-bait questions ("What do you think? 🤔")
- Never post without a concrete reference (number, project, producer, fact)
- Post as the founder personally, not as "FTB" brand account
- If a post could have been written by any AI/food/startup account, delete it

## Case Studies

### For Anthropic

**Tone:** Technical rigor + compelling narrative.

1. The Challenge (artisanal producer's commercial gap — 3-4 sentences)
2. The Approach (Claude as coordinator — architecture, prompts, human-AI loop)
3. The Experiment (timeline, methodology, what was measured)
4. Results (metrics, honest about limitations)
5. Key Insights (what Claude did well, where it struggled, surprises)
6. Implications (why this matters beyond one caseificio — the 300,000)

**What they want:** Claude solving real-world problems in a vertical nobody else covers. Visual, human, compelling. Not another SaaS chatbot.

### For UNISG

**Tone:** Academic rigor meets applied innovation.

1. Context (Italian artisanal food sector challenge — with data)
2. Research Question (framed academically)
3. Methodology (AI + domain expertise + human coordination)
4. Findings (results from projects)
5. Discussion (implications for food innovation education and research)
6. Collaboration Proposal (specific: workshop, joint research, curriculum integration)

**What they want:** A graduate applying their education in a novel way. Practical proof that gastronomic sciences + technology = real impact.

### For EU Evaluators

**Tone:** Impact-focused, data-driven, EU priorities aligned.

1. The Problem (European artisanal food SMEs losing competitiveness)
2. The Innovation (AI-native tools for artisanal production)
3. The Evidence (quantified project results)
4. The Team (founder + university + technology partner)
5. Scalability (one producer → national program)
6. Alignment (human-centric AI, Made in Italy, sustainability, digital SME transformation)

**What they want:** Measurable SME impact, EU AI Act alignment, university-industry collaboration, scalable model.

## Presentation Slides

### Core Deck (12 slides, customize per audience)

1. **Cover** — Cinematic food production photo + "FOOD TECH BOOTCAMP"
2. **The Number** — "300,000" on Terra Bruciata + context line
3. **The Problem** — Split screen: artisanal vs industrial
4. **The Statement** — Terra bg, Caglio text: "Non servono fabbriche più grandi. Servono strumenti più intelligenti."
5. **The Approach** — FTB as lab — what we do, how
6. **The Work (1)** — Project card with results
7. **The Work (2)** — Project card with results
8. **The Work (3)** — Project card with results
9. **The Ecosystem** — UNISG + Anthropic + EU + Producers
10. **The Founder** — Name, photo, credentials, story
11. **What's Next** — Current experiments + milestones
12. **Contact** — Clean. Email. One sentence.

### Audience Customization

- **Anthropic:** Expand slides 6-8 with Claude technical details
- **UNISG:** Add curriculum integration + research proposal slide
- **EU/Bandi:** Add impact metrics, sustainability, scalability slides
- **Producers:** Remove institutional content, focus on "here's what we did for someone like you"

## Editorial Calendar

| ---- | --------- | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| 1    | —         | Experiment update + sector insight | Update project page metrics |
| 2    | Blog post | Blog promotion + artisan story     | —                           |
| 3    | —         | Behind the scenes + data post      | Case study prep if needed   |
| 4    | —         | Manifesto post + week recap        | Monthly metrics review      |

### Content Debt Rule

If you skip a week, don't catch up with double output. Skip it. One good LinkedIn post per week beats a planned calendar you can't maintain.

## Publishing Checklist

Before publishing anything:

- [ ] Does it reference a real project, number, or producer?
- [ ] Are ≥2 artisanal sectors represented or implied?
- [ ] Would a producer feel respected reading this?
- [ ] Would an institutional partner take FTB seriously?
- [ ] Is technology secondary to the human story?
- [ ] Could a generic agency have written this? (If yes → rewrite)
