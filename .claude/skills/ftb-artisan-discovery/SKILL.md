# Artisan Discovery — Project Finding Skill

## Purpose

Guide the founder in discovering, qualifying, and scoping FTB projects through conversations with artisanal food producers. This is field research — the most important skill for a lab that builds from the production floor, not from a whiteboard.

---

## The Principle

FTB projects are not invented. They're discovered. In a caseificio at 4am. In a frantoio during pressing season. At a bodega during harvest. In a conversation where a producer says something that reveals a gap technology could fill.

Your job: listen for the gap. Never pitch. Never assume. Never arrive with a solution looking for a problem.

---

## The 5-Layer Interview Framework

Use these layers in sequence. Each layer goes deeper. Spend most time on Layers 2 and 3 — that's where projects hide.

### Layer 1: The Craft (5-10 min)

**Purpose:** Warm up. Let them be proud. Understand what makes them unique.

| Question                                                                | What you're listening for                                                            |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| "Tell me about your [product]. What makes it different?"                | Identity. What they emphasize = what they'd never automate.                          |
| "What did you learn from [previous generation] that no school teaches?" | Tacit knowledge. The stuff that could be lost. The stuff an LLM could help preserve. |
| "How many [units] do you produce per year?"                             | Scale. Limited production = high-value, fragile economics.                           |
| "What varieties/types do you work with?"                                | Complexity. More varieties = more data management needs.                             |
| "What's the hardest part of the process that nobody sees?"              | Hidden craft. Often where quality lives and where automation could help or hurt.     |

**Manrique note:** This layer is about respect. You're in their space. They're the expert. Start by honoring that.

### Layer 2: The Market (10-15 min)

**Purpose:** Understand commercial reality. Where do they sell? Where can't they reach?

| Question                                                                                                                     | What you're listening for                                                                                   |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Who buys your product today? How do they find you?"                                                                         | Channels. Direct, restaurants, export, retail, online, tourism? What's MISSING?                             |
| "Who's your biggest competitor? Not the best — the one who takes your clients."                                              | Competitive landscape. Industrial vs artisanal. Price vs quality.                                           |
| "If you could reach 100 [restaurants/shops/importers] in [city/country] who'd love your product, how would you do it today?" | Commercial bottleneck. The answer is usually: "I can't. No time, no tools, no people." THIS IS THE PROJECT. |
| "How do your clients reorder? What's the process?"                                                                           | Retention mechanics. Often manual, chaotic, easily improved.                                                |
| "Do you export? What stops you / what makes it hard?"                                                                        | Export = compliance + market intelligence + logistics. Multiple AI opportunities.                           |
| "How important is tourism/visits for your revenue?"                                                                          | If significant: booking, experience management, review generation — all solvable.                           |

### Layer 3: The Daily Pain (10-15 min)

**Purpose:** Find the operational gap. Where does time disappear?

| Question                                                                     | What you're listening for                                                                                                                          |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **"What takes the most time that has nothing to do with making [product]?"** | THE KILLER QUESTION. Answer is always: paperwork, compliance, accounting, social media, logistics, phone calls. Every answer is an AI opportunity. |
| "If you had 10 extra hours this week, what would you do?"                    | Their real priority. Where freed time would create most value.                                                                                     |
| "How do you manage orders, clients, follow-ups?"                             | "Excel." "WhatsApp." "My head." "A notebook." Each = project signal.                                                                               |
| "Does [DOP/DO/regulatory] compliance take much of your time?"                | Universal pain for protected designation producers. Automation is straightforward and high-value.                                                  |
| "How do you decide what to produce, how much, when?"                         | Production planning. Often gut-based. Data could help without replacing intuition.                                                                 |
| "How many hours per week do you spend on admin vs. production?"              | Quantified pain. Crucial for any future proposal or funding application.                                                                           |

### Layer 4: The Dream (5-10 min)

**Purpose:** Understand ambition and constraints. What project would actually fit?

| Question                                                              | What you're listening for                                                                                                                  |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"                               | Growth vs. stability. Some want to scale, some want to stay small but profitable, some want international recognition.                     |
| "If one tool could do ONE thing for your business, what would it be?" | Don't say "AI" or "technology." Say "uno strumento" / "una herramienta." Let them define the need.                                         |
| "What have you tried that didn't work?"                               | Previous technology failures. CRMs abandoned, websites never updated, social media attempts. Shows risk tolerance and what NOT to propose. |
| "Do you know other producers nearby facing similar challenges?"       | Network mapping. If there's a pattern, there's a scalable project.                                                                         |

### Layer 5: The Seed (5 min)

**Purpose:** Position yourself. Don't pitch — connect.

| Action                   | How                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Share your story briefly | "I studied gastronomic sciences at Pollenzo, then learned to code. Now I build AI tools for artisanal producers."                                                                                           |
| Reference Tosi           | "I work with a gorgonzola producer in Italy who had exactly this problem. We built an AI that..." — keep it casual, let them ask questions.                                                                 |
| Connect the philosophy   | "Your [workshop/cantina/frantoio] is exactly what I believe in — using the best modern tools to protect something that would otherwise be lost."                                                            |
| Don't pitch              | Plant the seed. If there's a project, it emerges naturally.                                                                                                                                                 |
| Exchange contacts        | Follow up with gratitude, not a proposal. "Thank you for the visit. Your [product] is extraordinary. If you ever want to explore how technology could help without changing what you do, I'd love to talk." |

## Project Signal Detection

### Strong Signals (High-probability project)

| They say...                                              | You hear...               | Project type                                      |
| -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| "I can't reach restaurants outside my area"              | Commercial bottleneck     | AI sales agent (Tosi model)                       |
| "I spend 2 days/week on paperwork"                       | Admin overload            | Compliance automation                             |
| "I don't know who buys my product after the distributor" | End-customer blindness    | CRM + customer intelligence                       |
| "My website doesn't bring any clients"                   | Digital presence failure  | NOT a website redesign — a lead generation system |
| "I have to explain every time why my product costs more" | Value communication gap   | AI-powered storytelling / content generation      |
| "Export regulations are a nightmare"                     | Compliance complexity     | Export intelligence + document automation         |
| "I can't find young people who want to learn this craft" | Knowledge transfer crisis | Knowledge capture / training LLM                  |

### Medium Signals (Needs investigation)

| They say...                                    | You hear...       | Next step                                                              |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "My costs keep going up"                       | Margin pressure   | Investigate: which costs? What data exists?                            |
| "Social media is important but I have no time" | Content gap       | Could be an AI content generator — but is this really their priority?  |
| "I'd like to sell online"                      | E-commerce desire | Investigate: do they have volume? logistics? Or is this a distraction? |
| "The younger generation isn't interested"      | Succession risk   | Investigate: is technology the answer or is it cultural?               |

### Weak Signals (Don't pursue yet)

| They say...                                     | Why it's weak                                                            |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| "I need a better website"                       | Usually not the real problem. Dig deeper.                                |
| "I need to be on TikTok"                        | Following trends, not addressing pain.                                   |
| "Everything is fine, we just need more clients" | Too vague. Needs Layer 3 deep dive.                                      |
| "Technology scares me"                          | Resistance signal — needs relationship building, not a project proposal. |

## Qualification Scorecard

After every producer conversation, score the opportunity:

```
PRODUCER: [Name]
LOCATION: [City, Region]
SECTOR: [Cheese / Wine / Oil / Bread / etc.]
PRODUCT: [Specific product(s)]
SCALE: [Production volume, team size, revenue estimate]

PAIN CLARITY: [1-5] How clearly can they articulate their problem?
URGENCY: [1-5] How much does this pain cost them today?
TECH READINESS: [1-5] Have they used any digital tools? Open to new ones?
BUDGET SIGNAL: [1-5] Can they invest? Or would they need funding?
FTB FIT: [1-5] Does this align with our mission and current capabilities?

TOTAL: [/25]

TOP PAIN POINT: [One sentence]
POTENTIAL PROJECT: [One sentence]
NEXT STEP: [Specific action]

NOTES: [Anything else — personal connection, interesting quotes, follow-up timing]
```

**Scoring guide:**

- 20-25: Strong opportunity. Propose a discovery sprint.
- 15-19: Promising. Build relationship, share relevant content, revisit in 1-2 months.
- 10-14: Interesting but not now. Stay connected, wait for trigger event.
- Below 10: Not a fit right now. No further investment of time.

## Field Kit — What to Bring

### Physical

- Phone (photos, voice notes, contact exchange)
- A small gift if appropriate (nothing expensive — a genuine gesture)
- Empty stomach (they will offer you product — always accept, always taste thoughtfully)

### Digital (Prepare Before Visit)

- Research the producer: website, social media, reviews, press mentions
- Check their DO/DOP/IGP status and what that implies
- Look at their competitors in the same denomination
- Note 2-3 specific things to ask about (shows genuine interest, not generic curiosity)

### Mental

- Curiosity > agenda
- Questions > answers
- Listening > talking (80/20 rule: they talk 80%, you talk 20%)
- Respect > efficiency (if they want to show you the aging room for 30 minutes, that IS the meeting)

---

## After the Visit

### Within 24 Hours

1. Send a thank-you message (WhatsApp or email — match their style)
2. Fill out the qualification scorecard (while memory is fresh)
3. Voice-memo yourself the top 3 insights (you'll forget details fast)
4. If they gave you product, photograph it and note tasting impressions

### Within 1 Week

1. If score > 15: Send one piece of relevant content (a blog post, an article about AI in their sector, a case study). NOT a pitch. Value first.
2. If clear project signal: Draft a 1-page concept note (problem → approach → expected outcome → next steps). Send ONLY if they expressed interest.
3. Add to your producer network map (name, sector, region, pain point, score, status).

### Never

- Send a generic proposal to someone who didn't ask for one
- Promise something you can't deliver with your current bandwidth
- Treat a conversation as a sales call — it's research, always

## Building the Network Map

Over time, conversations with producers create a map of the artisanal food sector's digital gaps. This map is valuable for:

- **EIT Food applications:** "We've interviewed X producers across Y sectors and identified these common challenges..."
- **UNISG proposals:** "Field research with Z artisanal producers reveals these gaps that applied technology research could address..."
- **Anthropic pitch:** "Based on conversations with producers across Italy and Europe, here are the top 5 use cases for Claude in artisanal food..."
- **Content:** Every conversation is a potential blog post, LinkedIn story, or case study seed

Track the map in a simple database:

```
| Producer | Sector | Region | Scale | Top Pain | Score | Status | Last Contact |
```

When you have 10+ entries, patterns emerge. Those patterns become FTB's research agenda.

## Adaptation by Sector

### Wine Producers (Like Bodega Erupción)

**Specific questions to add:**

- "How do you manage the relationship with the DO/denomination council?"
- "How do you handle wine tourism bookings and the visitor experience?"
- "Do you track fermentation data digitally or manually?"
- "How do you decide which restaurants to approach for wine placement?"
- "Is direct-to-consumer shipping part of your business? What's hard about it?"

### Cheese Producers

**Specific questions to add:**

- "How do you monitor aging conditions (temperature, humidity)?"
- "How do you manage the HACCP documentation?"
- "What's your relationship with the Consorzio?"
- "How do you decide when a wheel is ready?"

### Olive Oil Producers

**Specific questions to add:**

- "How do you decide the optimal moment for harvest?"
- "Do you track quality metrics (acidity, polyphenols) digitally?"
- "How do you differentiate your oil from commodity blends in the market?"

### Bakeries

**Specific questions to add:**

- "How do you manage production scheduling and waste?"
- "How do you handle ordering from regular clients (restaurants, shops)?"
- "Do you track ingredient costs and margins per product?"

_Every producer you talk to is a world. Every conversation is a potential project. But not every project is for now. The discipline is knowing which seed to plant and which to pocket for later. Tosi first. Everything else grows from proven results._
